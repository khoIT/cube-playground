Balancing view grain ↔ capture cadence

Capture cadence sets what history exists (the floor resolution); view grain only changes how it's displayed, and can't go finer than capture. Two ways to make that relationship visible instead of leaving the two controls disconnected.

Option A Capture stays in the header — Monitor view-grain becomes capture-aware Recommended

Keeps the single capture editor on the header pill (the decision we just shipped). The Monitor bar gains a read-only Captured · daily marker and disables view grains finer than capture (hatched), with a jump link to edit capture. One glance shows the cap; no second editor.

Header (unchanged): Live · daily ▾  —  the only place capture is edited.

Captureddaily → View grain

Finer grains are locked because capture is daily — they'd just carry forward flat. (jumps to the header pill)

Option B Co-locate both as a "Resolution" pair in the Monitor bar

Move capture editing down next to view grain so the two read as one unit: Capture every → View at. The header pill demotes to a read-only status badge. Strongest spatial link, but it relocates capture editing away from the header (reverses part of what we just shipped) and puts a write-control back in Monitor.

Header demotes to read-only: Live · daily (status only, not editable)

Capture every → View at

Editing Capture every here unlocks finer View at options live. Capture + view sit together; header is just a status read-out.
